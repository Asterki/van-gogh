1853, March 30

Vincent van Gogh is born in Groot-Zundert, Netherlands, to Theodorus van Gogh, a minister, and Anna Cornelia Carbentus.

1869-1876

Vincent works at Goupil & Cie, an art dealership in The Hague, London, and Paris.

1880-1881

Vincent studies art at the Royal Academy of Fine Arts in Brussels.

1881-1885

Vincent lives with his parents in Nuenen, Netherlands, and begins painting rural scenes and peasant life.

1886-1888

Vincent moves to Paris and lives with his brother Theo. He becomes influenced by Impressionist and Post-Impressionist artists and develops his distinctive style.

1888-1889

Vincent moves to Arles in the south of France, where he creates some of his most iconic works, including "The Starry Night" and "Sunflowers." He also experiences mental health issues and famously cuts off a portion of his ear.

1889-1890

After a breakdown, Vincent voluntarily admits himself to an asylum in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence. During this time, he continues to paint prolifically, producing some of his most celebrated works.

1890, July 27

Vincent van Gogh dies from a self-inflicted gunshot wound in Auvers-sur-Oise, France, at the age of 37.

1890-Present

Posthumously, Vincent van Gogh's artwork gains widespread recognition and acclaim, cementing his legacy as one of the most influential artists in history.

For Van Gogh, sunflowers held a symbolic significance beyond their botanical beauty. They represented the essence of vitality, resilience, and the fleeting yet radiant nature of existence. In his iconic series of sunflower paintings, Van Gogh sought to capture not just the visual likeness of the flowers, but their inner spirit – their luminous energy and transcendent beauty.

Through bold, expressive brushstrokes and a kaleidoscope of rich, luminous colors, Van Gogh transformed the sunflower into a symbol of hope, joy, and the eternal cycle of life. Each painting in the series reveals his deep emotional connection to the natural world and his relentless quest to convey the ineffable essence of existence through art.

Passion Vincent van Gogh's passion was an unstoppable force, an inferno burning brightly within him, igniting his soul and fueling his artistic vision. From an early age, Van Gogh was consumed by a fervent desire to express himself through art, driven by an insatiable hunger to capture the world's beauty and turmoil on canvas.

His passion manifested in every aspect of his life – in his relentless pursuit of artistic mastery, his unwavering commitment to his craft, and his willingness to sacrifice comfort and stability in the name of his art. Van Gogh lived and breathed painting, dedicating himself wholeheartedly to the pursuit of truth and beauty through his work.
